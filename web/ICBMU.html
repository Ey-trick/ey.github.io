The 5th International Conference on Bio-Medical Ultrasound (5th ICBMU) [第五届生物医学超声国际学术会议] organized by Huazhong University of Science and Technology (HUST) will be held on November 7-9, 2019 in Wuhan, China. It is co-organized by Chinese Association of Ultrasound in Medicine and Engineering and Hubei Province Association of Bio-medical Engineering.
 
Ultrasound is one of most active areas in medicine and biology, specially in clinic. It is the most often used medical equipment in the world and attracted for intensive attentions both in techniques and applications. ICBMU2019 will provide an interdisciplinary form on bio-medical ultrasound for the engineers, researchers and physicians. The topic of ICBMU2019 is THE NEW TECHNIQUES IN BIO-MEDICAL ULTRASOUND IMAGING AND NEW APPLICATIONS. In addition, artificial intelligence in medicine is another hot topic that many attendances may be interested in.
 
We are looking forward to meeting you in the very beautiful city Wuhan, China, during November 7-9, 2019. Definitely, 5th ICBMU will provide you a pleasant experience, new contacts and happy stay in Wuhan.

University and College

University


   The Huazhong University of Science and Technology (HUST) is a national key university directly under the administration of the Ministry of Education of P. R. China, and is among the first Universities joining the national “211 Project” and “985 Project”. It was founded on May 26th, 2000 as a result of the merger of the former Huazhong University of Science and Technology, Tongji Medical University and Wuhan Urban Construction Institute.

   HUST covers an area of over 1,153 acres with picturesque environment, elegant surroundings and has 72% greenery coverage, which has been honored as the “University in the Forest”. It offers its students and staff first-class logistic service, efficient network service and abundant library resources.

   HUST is a comprehensive and multi-disciplinary research university, covering 12 major disciplines: philosophy, economics, law, education, literature, history, engineering, agriculture, medicine, management and arts, offering a variety of degree programs, including 94 undergraduate programs, 225 master programs, and 184 PhD programs. There are 39 post-doctoral research centers, 7 national key first-level disciplines and 15 national key sub-disciplines. Three disciplines namely, Mechanical Engineering, Optical Engineering, and Public Health and Preventive Medicine ranks top 1 among its peers in China, while Electrical Engineering and Biomedical Engineering ranks 2nd and 3rd respectively. HUST also hosts various national advanced talent training bases for undergraduate education, including the “National Exemplary Software Faculty”, the “National Base of Life Science and Technological Talents”, the “National Base of IC Talents”, the “National Education Base of Basic Science Courses”, and the “School of Engineering Sciences”.




   HUST hosts around 3000 full-time faculty members, more than 900 of which are professors and 1300 associate professors. A large number of eminent scientists and professors are gathering here, including 15 academicians of the Chinese Academy of Science and the Chinese Academy of Engineering, 36 Thousand Talents Program members, 5 The One-Thousand Foreign Experts Program members, 52 The Thousand Talents Program for Distinguished Young Scholars members, 48 Changjiang Scholars Program for Distinguished Professors, 36 Changjiang Scholars Program for Visiting Professors, 56 winners of The National Science Fund for Distinguished Young Scholars, 15 Chief Scientists for 973 Program, 2 Chief Scientists for Key Research Programs, 3 Young Chief Scientists for 973 program, 27 winners of Research Fund for Distinguished Young Scientists, 224 winners of Program for New Century Excellent Talents in University, 39 Millions of Leading Engineering Talents, 9 National Teaching Masters.

   HUST is proud of her national laboratory--“Wuhan National Laboratory for Optoelectronics”(one of the six national labs in China), the National Science Center--“Wuhan National High Magnetic Field Center”, the Center for Gravitational Experiments and the biggest oxy-fuel combustion carbon capture pilot system in China—“3 MWth Carbon Capture Test Base”.

   In recent years, a number of high qualified academic papers cited by SCI, EI, ISTP and Medical Literature Analysis and Retrieval System (MEDLARS) have been published. The number of published articles is among the top ten universities in China. In 2014, 7 HUST research projects were awarded by the annual National Science and Technology Awards Ceremony.

   HUST has established partnerships with more than 100 renowned universities and research institutions worldwide. Over 1,500 scholars and experts visit and lecture here annually. Since 2013 the campus has welcomed several Nobel Laureates including Prof. Alan J. Heeger, the Nobel Laureate in Chemistry, and Prof. Klaus von Klitzing, German Nobel Prize winner in Physics. Currently around 3000 international students are studying at HUST. The students coming from different parts of the world gather here for degree and non-degree programs. Some courses are taught in Chinese while most of the degree programs are taught in English. Scholarships are accessible to all the applicants.

   The university has three affiliated hospitals, including Union Hospital, Tongji Hospital and Liyuan Hospital. The affiliated Union Hospital and Tongji Hospital are large modern comprehensive hospitals that integrate the functions of clinic medical service, teaching, scientific research and training. As the medical centers of Hubei Province and even Southern Central China, the hospitals maintain leading positions in organ transplantation, cardiovascular surgery, hematology, ENT, orthopedics, urology and Geriatric Conditions.

   Although the HUST did make a great progress in the past, it will keep on stepping forward with full vigor and vitality, aiming to gain its name as a world top-ranking university and create a brilliant future.


College


   The College of Life Science and Technology at HUST was founded in 1980 from the Department of Bioengineering.The College comprises two national key disciplines, biomedical engineering and biology. It has a complete curriculum program from Bachelor degree to Master and Ph.D. degrees and also a post-doctoral training station in the two national key disciplines. The college has a current enrollment of 2029 students including 1201 undergraduate students, 383 MS and 379 PhD students, and 155 international graduate students. It has the biomedical engineering as the national key discipline, the biophysics as the national priority program and the biology as the key discipline of Hubei Province. The College has been playing a key role in HUST in the research areas of biochemistry and biology, molecular biology and genetics, pharmacology and toxicology and agriculture sciences, which are ranked in the top 1% by international ESI.




   The college has a high emphasis on the collaboration oversea in research as well as student exchange programs such as United States, Sweden, Germany, Britain, Russia and Australia. Within the college, there are annual forums for MS students and conferences for Ph.D. candidates, cross-disciplinary innovative forums and multiple research salons. The college also invites well-known scientists and researchers to hold both domestic and international meetings.

   With the asset over 79 million yuan, the college is aiming to train the outstanding talents with confidence and lead innovative researches and applications in the highly integrated cross-discipline of basic and applied science, engineering and medicine to stay at the forefront of science and to address major national demands for social and economic grow.


Wuhan City

   Wuhan, the capital of Hubei Province, is a metropolis with beautiful natural scenery and long history and culture. The Yangtze river and the Han river meet in downtown Wuhan, dividing the central city into three towns facing each other across the river. Furthermore, Li Bai, a poet of the Tang Dynasty, wrote the poem “From the Yellow Crane Tower came the sound of jade flute; in May, Jiangcheng seems to see the falling plum blossoms.”. Therefore, Jiangcheng has become the laudatory name of Wuhan. As the name suggests, there are many rivers and lakes in Wuhan, and the East Lake is one of them.




   The East Lake scenic spot, located in the center of Wuhan, covers an area of 73 square kilometers, including 33 square kilometers of lake area. The East Lake is the second largest urban lake in China. In the scenic spot, there is an East Lake greenway with a total length of 28.7 kilometers. People can ride, run and walk on the East Lake greenway. There is also a cherry blossom garden in the scenic spot, which is named as the three largest cherry blossom gardens in the world together with the Hirosaki cherry blossom garden in Japan and the Washington cherry blossom garden in USA. The East Lake cherry blossom garden hosts the annual Cherry Blossom Festival from March to April every year.




   The Wuhan Yangtze River Bridge is the first bridge built on the Yangtze River and one of the landmark buildings in Wuhan. Built in 1957, the bridge spans Snake Mountain and Turtle Mountain with a total length of 1,670 meters. The bridge is divided into two floors, the upper floor is a highway with sidewalks on both sides, and the lower floor is a railway.

   The Hankou river beach, the largest river beach in Asia with an area of 1.6 million square meters, is a large cultural park near the Yangtze River, which integrates tourism, leisure, fitness, greening and entertainment. As night falls and lights light up on both sides of the riverbank, people walk along the cobbled paths, enjoying the rare leisure of a busy life.




   The Yellow Crane Tower has perched atop Snake Mountain for millennia and centuries, with white clouds wafting around and the Yangtze River surging below. This picturesque edifice vies for historic prestige with Yueyang Tower and Prince Teng Pavilion in Southern China. This masterwork blends classicism and modernism, poetics and aesthetics. Mounting the tower is a twofold luxury. You not only will enjoy inviting views, but also can experience a transcendental realm, in which mind and universe become one. Finally, the mind will be purified. All this is perhaps why the tower has showed off such a fascinating vibe over the past ages.




   There are many delicacies in Wuhan. The famous delicacies districts include Wuchang Hubu Lane, Huangpi Delicious Street and Hankou Jiqing Street. "Guo Zao" culture prevails in Wuhan, which means having breakfast. There are kinds of breakfast, such as hot and dry noodles, beef noodles, bean shreds, bean skin, noodle nests, fried buns, glutinous rice chicken, duck neck, soup bags, etc., which are enough to make people eat for a month without repeating.
